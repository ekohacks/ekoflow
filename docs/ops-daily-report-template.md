## Your Daily Routine

This routine exists for two reasons. First, it keeps you moving — you start each day knowing what to do instead of waiting to be told. Second, it makes your work visible. When you work remotely or independently, nobody sees you typing. Your commits, your Slack updates, and your reports are how the team knows work is happening. This is not about proving yourself. It is about staying connected.

### Morning Startup

- [ ] Open terminal. Run `git pull` on all your repos.
- [ ] Open Firefox on workspace 4. Check Slack. Respond to anything from overnight.
- [ ] Check Gmail. Respond to anything urgent.
- [ ] Post in Slack what you are working on today. One or two sentences. Example: "Today I am finishing the newsletter draft and setting up the student tracker CSV."
- [ ] Open your ops-90-day-plan.md on GitHub. Read the tasks for your current week. Know what you should be doing.

### During The Day

- [ ] Work on your tasks from the 90-day plan. One task at a time. Jumping between tasks feels productive but produces less. Finishing one thing is better than half-finishing three.
- [ ] Commit your work as you go. Every meaningful change gets its own commit. This is a habit — small commits make it easy to see what you did and undo what went wrong.
- [ ] If you are stuck for more than 15 minutes, do this in order: (1) re-read the guide in the docs folder, (2) search for the answer online, (3) if still stuck, set up a screen sharing call. The reason for this order: most of the time the answer is already in the docs. Searching builds the skill of finding answers yourself. Calling is for when the first two genuinely did not work.
- [ ] Practice your tools. If you are in Week 1, every time you open a file use Neovim. Every time you navigate folders use the terminal. Building fluency only happens through use.

### End of Day

- [ ] Push all your work. Run `git status` to make sure nothing is left uncommitted.
- [ ] Fill out the report below.
- [ ] Post a short summary (3 lines max) in Slack before you log off.
- [ ] Read tomorrow's tasks from the 90-day plan so you know what you are starting with in the morning.
