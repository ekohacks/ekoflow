## Your Daily Routine

Follow this order every day. Do not skip steps.

### Morning Startup

- [ ] Open terminal. Run `git pull` on all your repos.
- [ ] Open Firefox on workspace 4. Check Slack. Respond to anything from overnight.
- [ ] Check Gmail. Respond to anything urgent.
- [ ] Post in Slack what you are working on today. One or two sentences. Example: "Today I am finishing the newsletter draft and setting up the student tracker CSV."
- [ ] Open your ops-90-day-plan.md on GitHub. Read the tasks for your current week. Know what you should be doing.

### During The Day

- [ ] Work on your tasks from the 90-day plan. One task at a time. Do not jump between tasks.
- [ ] Commit your work as you go. Do not wait until end of day to commit everything at once. Every meaningful change gets its own commit.
- [ ] If you are stuck for more than 15 minutes, do this in order: (1) re-read the guide in the docs folder, (2) search for the answer online, (3) if still stuck, set up a screen sharing call. Do not send one-line questions on Slack.
- [ ] Practice your tools. If you are in Week 1, every time you open a file use Neovim. Every time you navigate folders use the terminal. No shortcuts through the GUI.

### End of Day

- [ ] Push all your work. Run `git status` to make sure nothing is left uncommitted.
- [ ] Fill out the report below.
- [ ] Post a short summary (3 lines max) in Slack before you log off.
- [ ] Read tomorrow's tasks from the 90-day plan so you know what you are starting with in the morning.
