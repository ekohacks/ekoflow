**Mindfulness is the spine of the Ekohacks way**

Shore's sentence is effectively our admissions policy. We do not filter for expertise. We filter for attention. Every practice we insist on is a mindfulness scaffold wearing a technical costume.

**Where it shows up in the dojo**

*Vim.* Every keystroke is a decision. You cannot drift through a modal editor the way you drift through VS Code with Copilot whispering. The tool forces presence.

*TDD.* Red, green, refactor is a breath cycle for code. State intent as a test. Write just enough. Pause. Improve. Vipassana with a compiler.

*No AI during training.* We remove the anaesthetic. Students feel the friction of thinking, which is the only muscle that grows a craftsman.

*No mocks.* Real collaborators make you confront the actual system rather than a comfortable fiction. You cannot be mindful of a stub.

*Pomodoro, prayer breaks, the mountain, the hiking, the kneeling chair.* We build silence into the day by design. Body and editor are one instrument.

**The metaphor**

A novice sushi chef serves excellent fish if he is fully awake. A bored expert cuts carelessly. Ekohacks bets on the awake apprentice every time.

**Why it matters commercially**

Our unit of value is not the certificate. It is attention, measured in commits, time to green, and AI consumption. We are not selling skill. We are selling a habit. Habits transfer to the workplace. Certificates do not.
